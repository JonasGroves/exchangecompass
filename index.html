Compatibility engine

Exchange compatibility score

Scored using sourced city data (QS World Rankings, real cost-of-living and city-characteristic research) — not guesswork. Pick what matters to you, then rank your top picks for a more personal result.

How the compatibility score works

Every university is scored 1–20 relative to all others on each factor you select. Your #1 priority carries the most weight — and each priority below it carries progressively less — so the order you rank things matters a lot.

Factors you can choose from: nightlife, beach access, nature & outdoors, culture, food scene, safety, English proficiency, city vibe, transit, low cost, academic ranking, city size, and proximity to home.

Budget: an exact budget match adds a 10% bonus. A close match adds 4%.

English courses: if you need English-taught classes and the university doesn't primarily offer them, the score is reduced by 35%.

The final number is a percentage. No university can score 100% — real top matches land around 75–90% depending on your priorities.

      Privacy
      How we handle your data

      What we collect
      When you submit a review, we store the text you write, your home university name, the exchange university you attended, the year, and any optional cost figures you provide. We do not collect your name, email address, or any identifying information unless you choose to include it in your review text.

      Where it's stored
      Reviews are currently stored in shared browser storage local to this prototype. In a future version, reviews will move to a proper database. No data is sent to external analytics services.

      Location data
      If you allow location access, your coordinates are used only to estimate flight distances and costs on your screen. They are never stored, logged, or sent anywhere.

      Cookies
      We do not use tracking cookies or third-party analytics. No advertising data is collected.

      Removal requests
      This is a student prototype. If you submitted a review and want it removed, the current mechanism is to clear your browser's local storage. A formal deletion flow will be added in a later version.

      About ExchangeCompass
      Built for students, by students

      The problem
      Finding honest information about student exchange is surprisingly hard. Official university pages show glossy photos. Reddit threads are scattered and hard to verify. Cost estimates are often wildly inaccurate. Students end up either over- or under-budgeting, and miss out on exchanges that would have suited them perfectly.

      What this is
      ExchangeCompass is a student-built platform with two layers: a base layer of real, sourced data (QS World Rankings, Numbeo city cost data, university accommodation offices, government visa cost estimates) and a review layer built up by students who've actually been on exchange.

      The dataset
      We currently list 548 universities across 79+ countries, each with verified cost data, a compatibility score system, and an academic timeline. Every cost figure has a named source.

      The compatibility engine
      The "Find Your Match" tool scores every university against your stated priorities using real per-city data — nightlife scores from city research, safety indices, beach proximity, QS academic rankings, EF English Proficiency Index scores — not guesswork or templated answers.

      Status
      This is an early prototype. Features are actively being added. Not affiliated with any university.
